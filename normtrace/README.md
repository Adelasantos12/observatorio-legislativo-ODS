# NormTrace-IHR

**Legal Internalisation Mapping Infrastructure for International Health Regulations**

---

## What is NormTrace-IHR?

NormTrace-IHR is an academic, open-methodology infrastructure for mapping international health law obligations onto domestic legal and institutional frameworks. It traces each obligation contained in the International Health Regulations (IHR) — from the 2005 version through the 2024 amendments adopted by WHA77.17 — against the legal, administrative, and institutional anchoring found within national legal systems.

The project applies a structured, reproducible analytical method to identify: where international obligations are reflected in domestic law; which actors hold formal competence; what procedures are in place; how coordination is organised across governmental levels; and where gaps, silences, or misalignments exist.

---

## What this project does — and does not — do

**NormTrace-IHR maps:**
- International obligations derived from IHR 2005 and IHR 2024 amendments
- The domestic legal and institutional basis against which each obligation can be anchored
- The responsible actors, competences, procedures, coordination mechanisms, and oversight structures
- The nature and degree of any gap between international obligation and domestic anchoring

**NormTrace-IHR does not:**
- Evaluate definitive legal compliance with international law
- Issue legal opinions or constitute legal advice
- Assess factual implementation capacity or programmatic performance
- Replace expert legal review of national legislation

---

## This version is a methodological pilot

This repository constitutes a **pilot** of the NormTrace-IHR methodology. Its purpose is to develop, test, and refine the analytical framework before scaling to additional countries or instruments.

**The outputs produced in this pilot are preliminary.** They are not intended as definitive assessments of legal compliance or implementation capacity. All outputs must be reviewed and validated by qualified legal experts before being used for policy, advocacy, or legal advice purposes.

---

## First pilot country: México

The first country under analysis is **México**. The pilot will map IHR obligations — including the 2024 amendments — against Mexican constitutional, statutory, regulatory, and administrative frameworks. Federal structure, coordination mechanisms, and rights-safeguard provisions will receive particular attention.
