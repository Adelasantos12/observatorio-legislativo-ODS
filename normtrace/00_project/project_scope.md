# Project Scope — NormTrace-IHR

**Version:** 0.1.0-pilot  
**Date:** 2026-05-05  
**Status:** Draft — subject to revision

---

## 1. Instrument base

**Primary instrument:** International Health Regulations (2005), third edition.  
Published by the World Health Organization. Entry into force: 15 June 2007.

**Amendment layer:** IHR 2024 amendments adopted by the World Health Assembly under resolution WHA77.17 (June 2024). These amendments modify or add provisions relating to: pandemic prevention and preparedness; equity; national IHR authorities; emergency procedures; and other areas. The analysis tracks both the original 2005 text and the changes introduced by the 2024 amendments.

---

## 2. Pilot country

**First pilot country: México**

Mexico is selected as the pilot country for the following reasons:
- Federal constitutional structure, which raises specific questions about the distribution of competences between federal and state levels for IHR implementation.
- Existence of a General Health Law (*Ley General de Salud*) and related regulatory framework that predates the IHR 2005 and has been partially updated since.
- Active participation in WHO governance processes.
- Availability of public legal texts in Spanish.

## 3. Unit of analysis

Each row in the mapping tables represents one analytical unit structured as follows:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `obligation_id` | Unique identifier for the IHR obligation (e.g., IHR_A6_1) |
| `ihr_article` | Article and paragraph in IHR 2005 or IHR 2024 amendment |
| `obligation_text` | Verbatim or closely paraphrased obligation |
| `obligation_type` | Category: notification / assessment / response / coordination / legal / rights / other |
| `domestic_legal_basis` | Constitutional, statutory, or regulatory provision(s) identified |
| `actor` | Institution(s) holding formal competence |
| `competence_type` | Exclusive / shared / concurrent / delegated / undefined |
| `procedure` | Applicable procedure (if identifiable in domestic law) |
| `coordination` | Coordination mechanism between governmental levels or agencies |
| `oversight` | Oversight, accountability, or judicial review mechanism |
| `anchoring_score` | Score 0–5 (see methodology_note.md) |
| `gap_type` | Type of gap identified (see methodology_note.md) |
| `notes` | Analyst notes, uncertainty flags, sources |
| `review_status` | preliminary / under_review / validated |

---

## 4. Temporal scope

- IHR 2005 obligations: as in force from 2007.
- IHR 2024 amendments: as adopted by WHA77.17; entry into force subject to State Party reservations and timelines.
- Mexican domestic law: instruments in force as of the date of analysis (recorded per file).

---

## 5. What is out of scope

- Evaluation of factual or programmatic implementation capacity.
- Judicial compliance assessment.
- Health system performance evaluation.
- IHR State Party Reports or Joint External Evaluation data analysis.
- Other international instruments (IHPN, Pandemic Agreement, etc.) — unless added in a later phase.
